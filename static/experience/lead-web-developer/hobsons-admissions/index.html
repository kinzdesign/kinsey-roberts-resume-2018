Hobsons Admissions

Lead Web Developer - Analyst/Programmer 3
Case Western Reserve University, Weatherhead School of Management, University Technology https://weatherhead.case.edu/ (External Link) 10900 Euclid Avenue Cleveland OH 44106 US

From mid-April to late-October 2017, my team was down a Business Analyst. This position helped 2 departments make efficient use of SaaS platforms. One of those platforms was a suite of Admission tools from Hobsons (now Campus Management). At the time, we were using the Connect CRM and the ApplyYourself online application.

My supervisor asked that I take over support of Hobsons while the position was vacant. She pitched this as an as-needed, as-possible basis and assured me that others would help fill the gap. The outgoing BA gave me some rough checklists, skeletal documentation and brief training. There were choruses of "you're good at figuring things out" and crossed fingers that it would be a slow summer.

Then, at one of my first admission meetings, they announced that we were launching a new degree program in August. This led to several rapid-fire thoughts:

  1. Relax! Wasn't there a checklist for this scenario?
  2. Wait, did it have any instructions? I don't seem to remember any…
  3. Maybe other people know how to do some of these things?
  4. Everyone keeps looking at me like they're expecting me to know how to do this.
  5. #&*%! The BA must have handled most of this in the past.

I took a few deep breaths to convince myself that this wasn't a stress-induced nightmare. No, this was reality and I would have to peel this onion. It took weeks of research to understand the full scope of work required to launch a new degree. At times it seemed that each question I answered prompted 3 more questions for me to research. I was in frequent contact with Hobsons as well as another school at CWRU that used their products. As the summer progressed, I would find solutions to other issues that arose and take notes. The minimalist checklists the BA left grew into pages of step-by-step directions. I take pride in leaving behind more documentation than I inherited.

"Wow, they told us that was impossible!" became a common response when I delivered solutions, including:

  • Streamlined the flow of English proficiency test scores and scholarship amounts
  • Created a sub-application with different essay prompts for a special program
  • Configured a new degree program by creating the necessary configuration and reports
  • Compiled data on a 150+ message email communication plan and presented it in a novel format
  • Converted 26 decision letters to a responsive email template
  • Integrated Live Chat product with Connect CRM to automate data flow
