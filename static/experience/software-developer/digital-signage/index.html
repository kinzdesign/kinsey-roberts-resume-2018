Digital Signage

Software Developer - Analyst/Programmer 2
Case Western Reserve University, Division of Student Affairs, IT Operations Group https://students.case.edu/ (External Link) 10900 Euclid Avenue Cleveland OH 44106 US

In 2005, CWRU opened 8 new residence halls and each building had an LCD info board in the lobby. A year later, the system was already showing its age. The consumer-grade TVs had developed permanent image retention from the mostly-static images. The attached computers had to be set to reboot each night because the software was unstable.

As a student employee in the summer of 2006, I was asked to look at options to improve or replace the existing system.

My initial research suggested that the software was not a good fit for our use case. My supervisor agreed with my conclusions and I got to work on a custom solution. I focused on consuming existing data sources, starting with simple RSS and iCal feeds. I went on to screen-scrape laundry machine statuses and shuttle bus arrival predictions. The most exotic integration was polling public printer statuses via SNMP. I went on to deliver 4 major versions of the software as I rose from student employee to Application Developer.

Administrators liked my software enough that they invested in adding displays across campus. The graph below shows its growth from an 8-display pilot to 36 installations and 2 campus TV stations. For most of those, I handled everything from writing the proposals to overseeing the installations. Switching to 24x7-rated LCD panels and varying the screen layout reduce image retention.
